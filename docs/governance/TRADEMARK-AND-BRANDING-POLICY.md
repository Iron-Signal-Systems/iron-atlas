## Purpose

The software license and the project branding boundary are separate.

Business Source License 1.1 permits the software uses stated in `LICENSE`; it
does not grant rights in the Iron Signal Systems name, Iron Atlas name, crest,
logos, taglines, or other source-identifying branding.

## Reserved branding

The following are reserved to the Licensor unless separately authorized:

- **Iron Signal Systems**
- **Iron Atlas**
- Iron Atlas crest and logo artwork
- “Built on purpose. Backed by discipline. Engineered to endure.”
- confusingly similar marks, visual identities, or product presentation

## Permitted factual reference

Accurate, non-misleading nominative references are permitted when reasonably
necessary to identify the original project, describe compatibility, provide
attribution, or explain that a work is derived from Iron Atlas.

Such reference must not imply sponsorship, certification, endorsement,
affiliation, official status, or commercial authorization.

## Forks and redistributed versions

A redistributed or modified version must preserve legally required license
notices. Unless separately authorized, it must also use a name and presentation
that clearly distinguish it from the official Iron Atlas product and avoid
customer or user confusion.

## Security and trust

Brand separation is part of the security boundary. An unofficial build must
not be presented in a way that could cause operators to mistake it for an
officially validated Iron Signal Systems release.

## No effect on license rights

This policy does not reduce rights expressly granted by the software license.
If this policy conflicts with rights that applicable law requires, applicable
law controls.

Questions about authorized commercial branding should be directed through
